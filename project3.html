3D & Lasercut Box

3D Printed Box

I created interlocking pieces that would snap together, creating a simple but efficient box. The box itself was printed in two parts, with cutouts on the front to accommodate the sensors/buttons.


My fusion design and first attempts at printing the box.

Once I'd printed the box attempts, I was able to edit my design to fit the alarm properly. On the third try, it fit perfectly, and snapped into place very nicely.

Various images of the completed 3d printed box.


Laser Cut Box

My next step was to turn the 3d design into something 2d. I had to design each side individually, extending the side, cutting it, and then adjusting the edges to allow for it to snap into the others. However, I didn't account that some of the sides would need to be a bit longer in order to snap into place, so while the box's edges do touch, they don't fit inside each other like it should. We printed the design multiple times as we figured out the power of the machine, and finally, I got six completed pieces. I then glued the pieces together to help them stay in place.
